Chapati Recipe

Description

Chapati is a soft, chewy, flaky flatbread that is a staple in Kenyan households especially during family gatherings and the Christman holidays.Every family has its own recipe on how to make the best chapatis.This recipe is an easy how-to guide on how to make soft and flaky chapatis that are sure to impress everyone.

Ingredients

  • Three (3) cups of all purpose flour sieved
  • TWo (2) tablespoon cooking oil
  • One (1) tablespoon sugar
  • Half (1/2) a tablespoon salt
  • One and a half cups warm water

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix flour, sugar and salt. Add cooking oil, mix with hands to form crumbs.
  2. Make a well and pour water bit by bit as you knead. Knead for atleast 5 mins, if the dough is sticky, add flour bit by bit as you knead.
  3. Continue kneading on your work surface for another 3 mins. Make sure to knead thoroughly for soft chapatis
  4. Finally dough should be firm but soft and not sticky. Cover with a damp cloth/ cling film/plastic polythene bag for 30 minutes to allow the dough to rest.
  5. After 30mins, knead again and cut into balls.
  6. Roll out the balls in a circular shape with a rolling pin, one at a time and brush with oil on the surface then proceed to roll and fold into circular balls
  7. Cover the balls in a clean cloth to ensure they become soft
  8. Roll the balls out again forming the circle.Layers should be visible on the rolled out dough.
  9. In a medium heat, place your skillet, let it warm up. Then put your chapati ontop. Fry both sides until brown. Once cooked remove and place it in a container and cover.
  10. Serve with your favourite stew or beverage
